🛡️ Case Study: Cyberbullying of a College Student



**Introduction:-**

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where an individual is targeted through social media, messaging apps, or other digital platforms. It can include abusive messages, spreading rumors, or sharing embarrassing content. With increased online activity, such incidents are becoming more common among students.



**Background of the Case:-**

Riya, a first-year college student, regularly used social media to stay connected with her friends. One day, after a small misunderstanding with a classmate, she started receiving negative comments on her posts. Soon, one particular individual began targeting her repeatedly through messages and public comments.



**How the Crime Happened:-**

-A minor disagreement occurred between Riya and a classmate.
-The individual started posting rude and offensive comments on her social media posts.
-The bully sent personal messages containing hurtful and insulting language.
-Edited images and false rumors about Riya were shared online.
-The content spread among other students, increasing the impact.
-The harassment continued over time, affecting her daily life.



**Targeted Victim:-**

Riya, like many college students, was active online and became an easy target due to a personal conflict and regular social media presence.



**Consequences:-**

As a result of the cyberbullying, Riya experienced stress, anxiety, and a drop in confidence. She became less active on social media and started avoiding interactions with classmates. Her academic focus was also affected, and she felt emotionally disturbed.



**Conclusion:-**

This case shows how cyberbullying can seriously impact an individual’s mental well-being. It is important to use social media responsibly, avoid engaging in harmful behavior, and report such incidents to create a safer online environment.
